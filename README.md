# HR-Analysis-Report

![headcount](images/headcount)

**HR Executive Insights (2012–2023)**
- Workforce Overview
- Total Headcount: 3K employees
- Turnover Rate: 42.77% 
- Majority of employees are Individual Contributors

  ![department](images/departed)

**Department Insights**
- Largest departments: Software (783) and Sales (635)

- Departures are highest in Sales and Software

- Turnover is concentrated in revenue-generating functions

**Workforce Composition**

- Gender split: ~61% Male, ~39% Female
- Majority age range: 30–45 years
- 71% of employees are Single
- Most employees hold a Bachelor’s degree

**Location & Work Mode**

- Majority work Remote
- San Francisco and Los Angeles have the highest headcount concentration
- Departures also heavily linked to remote workforce

**Top termination reasons:**

- Better salary
- More fleixibility
- Found a better job

Turnover appears compensation and career-growth driven, not performance-driven.

Management Structure

Workforce heavily weighted toward Individual Contributors (74%)

Smaller proportion of managers and directors

Indicates flat organizational structure

Overall Insight

The organization has strong talent concentration in technical and sales functions but faces elevated turnover driven primarily by external opportunities and compensation competition. Retention strategy should focus on career growth and pay competitiveness.
